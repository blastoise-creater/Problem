AquaSentry Construction & Materials Guide

Core Components

The AquaSentry device integrates multiple sensors and microcontrollers for accurate real-time analysis. Major parts include: water quality sensors (pH, turbidity, TDS, temperature), Arduino UNO, OLED display, and solar panel power unit.

Construction & Setup

The sensors are mounted near the tap outlet for direct measurement. All components are secured inside a waterproof plastic casing. The solar panel on the top powers the system, while internal wiring connects sensors to the microcontroller.

Cost & Maintenance

Each unit costs approximately ₹3500–₹4000. The system requires minimal maintenance: cleaning the sensors monthly and checking battery health quarterly ensures optimal function. Data can be stored for up to a year using a microSD module.

Design & Safety

The device is enclosed in a waterproof, non-corrosive plastic box. The transparent front protects the OLED screen and LEDs. Internal circuits are insulated to prevent shorting, ensuring full safety in rural and urban conditions alike.
